# Membership Renewal FAQ

## When does my membership expire?
Your membership expires on your annual renewal date, which is 12 months from when you first joined or last renewed. You can find your renewal date in your APhA account profile at pharmacist.com.

## How do I renew my membership?
You can renew online at pharmacist.com/renew. Log in to your account and follow the renewal steps. You can pay by credit card, check, or purchase order.

## Can I renew early?
Yes. You can renew up to 90 days before your expiration date. Your renewal period will begin after your current membership ends — you won't lose any time.

## What happens if I let my membership lapse?
You will lose access to all member benefits immediately upon expiration, including CPE access, journal subscriptions, and member pricing for events. You can reinstate at any time by renewing online.

## Can I switch tiers at renewal?
Yes. If you were a student and have graduated, you should switch to New Practitioner at renewal. If your New Practitioner period has ended (3 years), you move to full Pharmacist membership. Contact APhA member services if you need help with tier changes.

## Do you offer payment plans?
APhA does not currently offer installment plans for individual memberships. Corporate and group memberships may have different arrangements — contact APhA for details.

## Is there a group or institutional membership?
Yes. APhA offers group memberships for pharmacy schools, hospital systems, and pharmacy chains. Contact APhA Member Services for group pricing.

## How do I cancel my membership?
Contact APhA Member Services at memberservices@aphanet.org or 1-800-237-APhA. Memberships are non-refundable after 30 days.

## I'm a retired pharmacist — is there a discount?
Yes. Contact APhA Member Services to ask about retired pharmacist membership options.
