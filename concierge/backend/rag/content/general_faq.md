# General APhA FAQ

## What is APhA?
The American Pharmacists Association (APhA) is the largest association of pharmacists in the United States, founded in 1852. APhA represents more than 63,000 pharmacists, student pharmacists, pharmacy technicians, and pharmaceutical scientists. APhA advocates for pharmacists' roles in patient care and provides education, publications, and resources.

## What is the difference between APhA and my state pharmacy association?
APhA is the national organization. Your state pharmacy association (e.g., CPhA in California, TPA in Texas) advocates at the state level and handles state-specific licensure issues. Many pharmacists belong to both. APhA and state associations often collaborate on advocacy.

## Does APhA accredit pharmacy schools?
No. Pharmacy school accreditation is handled by ACPE (Accreditation Council for Pharmacy Education), a separate organization. APhA does, however, accredit CPE programs for continuing education.

## Can I join APhA if I am not a pharmacist?
Yes. APhA offers Supporter and Associate memberships for non-pharmacists who work in healthcare, industry, or support the pharmacy profession. Pharmacy technicians have their own membership tier.

## How do I access my member benefits after joining?
After joining, you will receive a welcome email with your login credentials. Log in at pharmacist.com/login to access all benefits including the Learning Library, journals, and ENGAGE community.

## What is APhA-ASP?
APhA-ASP (Academy of Student Pharmacists) is APhA's student division. It is the largest student pharmacy organization in the US. Most pharmacy schools have an APhA-ASP chapter. Student APhA membership automatically includes APhA-ASP membership.

## Is my APhA membership tax deductible?
APhA is a 501(c)(6) organization. Membership dues paid to a professional association may be deductible as a business expense — consult your tax advisor for specifics.
