# Amoxicillin

**Drug Class:** Aminopenicillin (Beta-Lactam Antibiotic)  
**Brand Names:** Amoxil, Trimox, Moxatag (ER)  
**APhA Reference Version:** 2024.1

---

## Mechanism of Action

Amoxicillin is a bactericidal aminopenicillin that inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs). This inhibits transpeptidation (cross-linking) of peptidoglycan strands, leading to osmotic instability and cell lysis. It is active against gram-positive cocci and some gram-negative organisms but is susceptible to hydrolysis by beta-lactamases.

---

## Spectrum of Activity

| Category | Organisms |
|----------|-----------|
| Gram-positive | *Streptococcus pneumoniae, S. pyogenes, Enterococcus faecalis, Listeria monocytogenes* |
| Gram-negative | *Haemophilus influenzae* (non-beta-lactamase producing), *Escherichia coli, Helicobacter pylori, Neisseria gonorrhoeae* (non-PPNG) |
| Limited activity | MRSA, beta-lactamase-producing organisms, *Pseudomonas* — NOT covered |

---

## Indications

- **FDA-Approved:**
  - Ear/nose/throat infections (including acute otitis media)
  - Lower respiratory tract infections (community-acquired pneumonia, bronchitis)
  - Genitourinary tract infections (uncomplicated UTI)
  - Skin and soft tissue infections
  - Helicobacter pylori eradication (as part of combination therapy)
  - Dental/oropharyngeal infections (endocarditis prophylaxis per AHA)

## Dosing

### Adults — Standard Dosing

| Indication | Dose | Frequency | Duration |
|------------|------|-----------|----------|
| Mild–moderate infections | 250–500 mg | Every 8 h | 7–10 days |
| Severe infections | 875 mg | Every 12 h | 7–14 days |
| Community-acquired pneumonia (mild) | 1,000 mg | Every 8 h | 5–7 days |
| Acute otitis media | 500 mg | Every 12 h | 5–7 days (adults); 10 days (severe) |
| *H. pylori* (triple therapy) | 1,000 mg | Every 12 h | 10–14 days (with clarithromycin + PPI) |
| Pharyngitis (Group A Strep) | 500 mg | Every 12 h OR 250 mg every 8 h | 10 days |
| SBE prophylaxis | 2,000 mg | Single dose 30–60 min before procedure | Single dose |

### Pediatric Dosing

| Indication | Dose | Frequency |
|------------|------|-----------|
| Mild–moderate | 25 mg/kg/day | Divided every 12 h |
| Severe; pneumonia | 40–45 mg/kg/day | Divided every 12 h |
| Acute otitis media (high dose) | 80–90 mg/kg/day | Divided every 12 h |
| Pharyngitis | 50 mg/kg/day | Once daily (max 1,000 mg/day) |
| Maximum single dose | 500 mg | — |

### Renal Dose Adjustment

| CrCl | Recommendation |
|------|----------------|
| >30 mL/min | No adjustment |
| 10–30 mL/min | Max 500 mg every 12 h |
| <10 mL/min | 250–500 mg every 24 h |
| Hemodialysis | Dose after dialysis |

## Contraindications

- Hypersensitivity to amoxicillin or other beta-lactam antibiotics (penicillins, cephalosporins — cross-reactivity ~1–2% for cephalosporins)
- Previous anaphylaxis to any penicillin

---

## Warnings and Precautions

### Hypersensitivity Reactions
Range from maculopapular rash (common; 5–10%) to urticaria, angioedema, anaphylaxis (rare but potentially fatal). Ask about penicillin allergy before prescribing. Anaphylaxis requires epinephrine.

### *C. difficile* Colitis (CDAD)
Antibiotic use disrupts gut flora and may cause *Clostridioides difficile* colitis. Consider in patients with diarrhea during or after treatment.

### Mononucleosis Rash
Aminopenicillins (amoxicillin, ampicillin) cause a distinctive non-allergic diffuse maculopapular rash in ~90% of patients with Epstein-Barr virus (EBV) mononucleosis. This rash does NOT indicate penicillin allergy but requires distinguishing from true allergic reactions.

### Superinfection
Prolonged use may allow overgrowth of non-susceptible organisms (*Candida*, *C. difficile*, MRSA).

## Drug Interactions

| Interacting Drug | Effect | Management |
|-----------------|--------|------------|
| Warfarin | Altered INR (rare GI flora disruption) | Monitor INR |
| Oral contraceptives | Historically feared (theoretical GI flora); evidence does not support significant interaction | No action required; counsel |
| Probenecid | Reduces renal tubular secretion → ↑ amoxicillin levels | Used therapeutically in some protocols |
| Allopurinol | Increased risk of rash | Avoid combination if possible |
| Methotrexate | Reduced renal clearance of methotrexate | Monitor for methotrexate toxicity |

## Adverse Effects

| Effect | Frequency |
|--------|-----------|
| Diarrhea | 3–10% |
| Nausea, vomiting | Common |
| Skin rash (maculopapular) | 5–10% (higher with EBV) |
| Urticaria | Uncommon |
| *C. difficile* colitis | Rare |
| Anaphylaxis | Rare (<0.05%) |
| Elevated liver enzymes | Uncommon |

## Pharmacokinetics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Bioavailability | 74–92% (excellent oral absorption; not significantly affected by food) |
| Tmax | 1–2 h |
| Protein binding | ~18% |
| Metabolism | Not significantly metabolized; ~30% degraded in GI tract |
| Half-life | ~1–1.5 h (prolonged in renal impairment) |
| Elimination | Renal (60–80% unchanged in urine) |

---

## Monitoring Parameters

- Signs/symptoms of therapeutic response (resolution of fever, pain, erythema)
- Signs of hypersensitivity (rash, urticaria, angioedema, respiratory symptoms)
- Diarrhea (assess for *C. difficile* if severe, persistent, or bloody)
- Renal function in patients with impairment

---

## Patient Counseling Points

1. Take all doses as prescribed, even if you feel better — stopping early can allow bacteria to regrow.
2. May be taken with or without food; food may help reduce stomach upset.
3. Refrigerate the liquid suspension; shake well before each dose; discard unused portion after 14 days.
4. A skin rash that appears after 2–3 days of use (flat, blotchy, non-raised) does NOT necessarily indicate a penicillin allergy — contact your pharmacist/provider for evaluation.
5. Seek immediate medical care for hives, difficulty breathing, or swelling of face/throat.
6. Report severe diarrhea (watery, bloody, or accompanied by fever/cramping) — may indicate *C. difficile* infection.
7. Complete the full course as prescribed.

## Common Clinical Pitfalls

1. **High-dose for AOM:** Standard community-acquired *S. pneumoniae* now requires 80–90 mg/kg/day for middle ear tissue concentrations; standard doses may be inadequate.
2. **Don't use for viral infections:** Amoxicillin has no activity against viruses — inappropriate for viral URI, influenza.
3. **EBV rash ≠ penicillin allergy:** Document clearly to avoid future penicillin avoidance.
4. **Duration matters:** Group A strep pharyngitis requires full 10 days to prevent rheumatic fever recurrence.
