# Atorvastatin Calcium

**Drug Class:** HMG-CoA Reductase Inhibitor (Statin)  
**Brand Names:** Lipitor  
**APhA Reference Version:** 2024.1

---

## Mechanism of Action

Atorvastatin competitively inhibits HMG-CoA reductase, the rate-limiting enzyme in cholesterol biosynthesis. This reduces intracellular cholesterol, upregulates LDL receptor expression on hepatocytes, increases LDL clearance from plasma, and decreases VLDL production. Atorvastatin also reduces triglycerides and modestly increases HDL-C. It demonstrates pleiotropic effects (anti-inflammatory, endothelial stabilization) that may contribute to cardiovascular risk reduction beyond lipid lowering.

---

## Indications

- **FDA-Approved:**
  - Primary hyperlipidemia and mixed dyslipidemia (heterozygous familial and non-familial)
  - Homozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (HoFH)
  - Primary prevention of cardiovascular events (patients with multiple risk factors)
  - Secondary prevention of cardiovascular events (established CVD)
  - Hypertriglyceridemia
  - Pediatric heterozygous familial hypercholesterolemia (≥10 years)

---

## Dosing

### Adults
| Intensity | Dose | Expected LDL-C Reduction |
|-----------|------|--------------------------|
| Moderate-intensity | 10–20 mg/day | ~30–50% |
| High-intensity | 40–80 mg/day | ≥50% |

- **ASCVD secondary prevention:** High-intensity preferred (40–80 mg/day)
- **Primary prevention, high risk:** High-intensity preferred
- **Titrate** based on lipid response and tolerability; reassess in 4–12 weeks

### Pediatric (Heterozygous FH, ≥10 years)
- Start: 10 mg/day; maximum: 20 mg/day

### Renal Adjustment
No dose adjustment required (not renally cleared).

### Hepatic Impairment
Contraindicated in active liver disease or unexplained persistent transaminase elevations.

---

## Contraindications

- Active liver disease or unexplained persistent elevations of hepatic transaminases
- Pregnancy (teratogenic — cholesterol biosynthesis critical for fetal development)
- Breastfeeding
- Hypersensitivity to atorvastatin

---

## Warnings and Precautions

### Myopathy / Rhabdomyolysis
Dose-related risk of muscle toxicity ranging from myalgia to rhabdomyolysis. Risk increased by:
- High-dose therapy
- Advanced age (>65 years)
- Hypothyroidism (untreated)
- Renal impairment
- Drug interactions (see below)

Instruct patients to promptly report unexplained muscle pain, tenderness, or weakness, especially with fever or dark/brown urine.

### Hepatotoxicity
Rare; persistent transaminase elevations (>3× ULN) require dose reduction or discontinuation. Routine monitoring not required in absence of symptoms.

### Immune-Mediated Necrotizing Myopathy (IMNM)
Rare autoimmune complication; anti-HMGCR antibodies; requires immunosuppressive therapy.

### Diabetes Risk
Modest increase in HbA1c and diabetes incidence; benefit of CVD reduction generally outweighs risk.

## Drug Interactions

| Interacting Drug | Mechanism | Effect | Recommendation |
|-----------------|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Cyclosporine | OATP1B1/CYP3A4 inhibition | ↑ atorvastatin AUC ~8.7× | Contraindicated (max 10 mg if unavoidable) |
| Clarithromycin, itraconazole | Strong CYP3A4 inhibition | ↑ atorvastatin levels | Max 20 mg; consider alternative antibiotic/antifungal |
| HIV protease inhibitors (lopinavir, ritonavir) | CYP3A4 inhibition | ↑ atorvastatin AUC | Max 20 mg |
| Gemfibrozil | Unknown | ↑ myopathy risk | Avoid combination if possible |
| Niacin (≥1 g/day) | Pharmacodynamic | ↑ myopathy risk | Use with caution |
| Rifampin | CYP3A4 induction | ↓ atorvastatin levels | Administer simultaneously (not sequentially) |
| Colchicine | Pharmacodynamic | ↑ myopathy risk | Use with caution |

## Adverse Effects

| Effect | Frequency |
|--------|-----------|
| Myalgia | 5–10% |
| Nasopharyngitis | Common |
| Arthralgia | Common |
| Diarrhea, dyspepsia | Common |
| Elevated liver enzymes | 0.5–2% |
| Rhabdomyolysis | Rare (<0.1%) |
| New-onset diabetes | Small absolute increase |

---

## Pharmacokinetics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Bioavailability | ~14% (extensive first-pass) |
| Tmax | 1–2 h |
| Protein binding | ≥98% |
| Metabolism | CYP3A4 (active metabolites) |
| Half-life | ~14 h (activity 20–30 h due to active metabolites) |
| Elimination | Biliary/fecal (~70%); renal (<2%) |

---

## Monitoring Parameters

- Fasting lipid panel: 4–12 weeks after initiation or dose change; then annually
- Liver function tests: At baseline; if symptoms of hepatotoxicity develop
- CK: At baseline (high-risk patients); if myopathy symptoms occur
- Blood glucose / HbA1c: In patients at risk for diabetes
- Thyroid function (TSH): If myopathy occurs without other explanation

---

## Timing and Administration

- May be taken at any time of day, with or without food
- No requirement to take at bedtime (unlike shorter-acting statins like simvastatin)
- Consistent daily administration maximizes effect

---

## Patient Counseling Points

1. Take once daily; food does not affect absorption.
2. Promptly report unexplained muscle pain, weakness, or tenderness — especially if accompanied by fever, fatigue, or dark/brown urine.
3. Do not take if pregnant or planning to become pregnant; use effective contraception.
4. Avoid large amounts of grapefruit juice (inhibits CYP3A4; may modestly increase atorvastatin levels).
5. Inform all healthcare providers and pharmacists about atorvastatin use before starting new medications.
6. This medication reduces cardiovascular risk; continue even if you feel well.
