# Clinical Guidelines: Anticoagulation Therapy

**Source:** CHEST Antithrombotic Therapy Guidelines (2016, updated recommendations 2021–2022)  
**Supplemented by:** AHA/ACC Atrial Fibrillation Guidelines 2023; ASH VTE Guidelines 2020  
**APhA Reference Version:** 2024.1

## Overview of Anticoagulant Drug Classes

### Vitamin K Antagonists (VKA)
- **Warfarin** (Coumadin, Jantoven): Only VKA available in the US
- Mechanism: Inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase → reduced carboxylation of factors II, VII, IX, X, protein C, S
- Monitoring: INR (therapeutic range typically 2.0–3.0 or 2.5–3.5)

### Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs)

#### Direct Thrombin Inhibitors (DTI)
| Drug | Brand | Target |
|------|-------|--------|
| Dabigatran | Pradaxa | Factor IIa (thrombin) — direct |

#### Factor Xa Inhibitors
| Drug | Brand | Target |
|------|-------|--------|
| Rivaroxaban | Xarelto | Factor Xa — direct |
| Apixaban | Eliquis | Factor Xa — direct |
| Edoxaban | Savaysa | Factor Xa — direct |
| Betrixaban | Bevyxxa | Factor Xa — extended prophylaxis |

### Parenteral Anticoagulants
| Drug | Class | Route | Monitoring |
|------|-------|-------|-----------|
| Unfractionated Heparin (UFH) | Anti-Xa/IIa (indirect) | IV/SQ | aPTT or anti-Xa |
| Enoxaparin (Lovenox) | LMWH (anti-Xa > IIa) | SQ | Anti-Xa (selected patients) |
| Dalteparin (Fragmin) | LMWH | SQ | Anti-Xa (selected patients) |
| Fondaparinux (Arixtra) | Synthetic pentasaccharide (anti-Xa indirect) | SQ | Usually none |
| Argatroban | DTI | IV | aPTT |
| Bivalirudin | DTI | IV | aPTT or ACT |

## Atrial Fibrillation — Stroke Prevention

### Risk Stratification: CHA₂DS₂-VASc Score

| Risk Factor | Points |
|------------|--------|
| **C** – Congestive heart failure (or EF ≤40%) | 1 |
| **H** – Hypertension | 1 |
| **A₂** – Age ≥75 years | **2** |
| **D** – Diabetes mellitus | 1 |
| **S₂** – Stroke/TIA/thromboembolism history | **2** |
| **V** – Vascular disease (prior MI, PAD, aortic plaque) | 1 |
| **A** – Age 65–74 years | 1 |
| **Sc** – Sex category (female) | 1 |
| **Maximum score** | **9** |

### Treatment Recommendation Based on CHA₂DS₂-VASc

| Score | Men | Women | Recommendation |
|-------|-----|-------|----------------|
| 0 | 0 | — | Anticoagulation NOT recommended |
| 1 | 1 | 0 | Consider anticoagulation (score based on non-sex risk factors) |
| ≥2 | ≥1 (after subtracting female sex point) | ≥2 | Anticoagulation **recommended** |

*Female sex alone (score 1) does not warrant anticoagulation — only modifies risk when another risk factor is present*

### Bleeding Risk: HAS-BLED Score

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| **H** – Hypertension (uncontrolled, SBP >160) | 1 |
| **A** – Abnormal renal or liver function | 1 each |
| **S** – Stroke history | 1 |
| **B** – Bleeding history or predisposition | 1 |
| **L** – Labile INR (warfarin TTR <60%) | 1 |
| **E** – Elderly (age >65) | 1 |
| **D** – Drugs (antiplatelets, NSAIDs) or alcohol | 1 each |

*Score ≥3 = high bleeding risk; use to identify and correct modifiable bleeding risk factors, NOT to withhold anticoagulation if stroke risk is high*

### Drug of Choice for AF

**DOACs preferred over warfarin** (all major AF trials: RE-LY, ROCKET-AF, ARISTOTLE, ENGAGE AF):
- Lower intracranial hemorrhage risk
- No routine INR monitoring
- Fewer drug/food interactions
- Equal or superior stroke/systemic embolism prevention

**Warfarin preferred:**
- Mechanical heart valves
- Rheumatic mitral stenosis
- CrCl <15–25 mL/min (DOAC not adequately studied)
- Triple-positive antiphospholipid antibody syndrome
- Cost/access barriers to DOACs

## Venous Thromboembolism (VTE) — DVT and PE

### Initial Treatment

| Setting | Preferred |
|---------|-----------|
| DVT (distal, low risk) | Consider surveillance ultrasound over anticoagulation |
| DVT (proximal) | Anticoagulate 3+ months |
| PE (low risk) | Anticoagulate; consider outpatient treatment |
| PE (intermediate risk/massive) | Anticoagulate; consider thrombolytics or catheter intervention |
| Massive PE (obstructive shock) | Systemic thrombolysis if no contraindication |

### DOAC Dosing for VTE Treatment

| Drug | Acute Phase | Maintenance |
|------|------------|-------------|
| Rivaroxaban | 15 mg BID × 21 days | 20 mg once daily with evening meal |
| Apixaban | 10 mg BID × 7 days | 5 mg BID |
| Dabigatran | Parenteral anticoagulation × 5–10 days first | 150 mg BID |
| Edoxaban | Parenteral anticoagulation × 5–10 days first | 60 mg once daily (30 mg if weight ≤60 kg or CrCl 15–50 or P-gp inhibitor) |
| Warfarin | Overlap heparin/LMWH ≥5 days until INR ≥2 for 24 h | 2.0–3.0 INR |

### Duration of Anticoagulation

| VTE Scenario | Duration |
|-------------|----------|
| Provoked DVT/PE (surgery, trauma, immobilization — transient) | 3 months |
| Provoked by hormonal therapy/pregnancy | 3 months (or until delivery + 6 weeks for pregnancy) |
| Unprovoked proximal DVT or PE — first event | Minimum 3–6 months; reassess for extended therapy |
| Unprovoked DVT/PE — low/moderate bleeding risk | Extended therapy (indefinite) preferred if patient accepts |
| Cancer-associated VTE | Ongoing; anticoagulate as long as cancer is active |
| Recurrent unprovoked VTE | Extended indefinite therapy |
| Antiphospholipid antibody syndrome | Indefinite (warfarin preferred over DOACs for triple-positive) |

### Extended Therapy at Reduced Dose

After completion of 6+ months of standard-dose anticoagulation for unprovoked VTE:
- **Rivaroxaban 10 mg once daily** (EINSTEIN CHOICE): Reduces recurrent VTE vs aspirin with similar bleeding
- **Apixaban 2.5 mg BID** (AMPLIFY-EXT): Reduces recurrent VTE vs placebo without increased major bleeding

## Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia (HIT)

### Clinical Recognition (4T Score)

| Factor | 2 Points | 1 Point | 0 Points |
|--------|----------|---------|---------|
| Thrombocytopenia | >50% fall or nadir ≥20 | 30–50% fall or nadir 10–19 | <30% fall or nadir <10 |
| Timing of platelet fall | Days 5–10 or ≤1 day with prior heparin 30–100 days | Beyond day 10 or timing unclear | ≤1 day with no recent heparin |
| Thrombosis or other sequelae | New thrombosis, skin necrosis | Progressive or recurrent thrombosis | None |
| Other causes of thrombocytopenia | None apparent | Possible | Definite |

| 4T Score | Probability | Action |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| 0–3 | Low (<5%) | Continue heparin; investigate other causes |
| 4–5 | Intermediate (~14%) | Discontinue heparin; send anti-PF4/heparin antibody test; bridge with non-heparin anticoagulant |
| 6–8 | High (~64%) | Discontinue heparin immediately; start alternative anticoagulant; send confirmatory testing |

### Alternative Anticoagulants for HIT
- **Argatroban** (DTI; preferred in renal failure): IV drip; monitor aPTT
- **Bivalirudin** (DTI): IV; preferred in cardiac procedures or renal failure
- **Fondaparinux** (off-label; anti-Xa): May be used in stable patients
- **DOACs** (off-label for acute HIT; limited data): Emerging evidence

## Perioperative Anticoagulation Management

### Bridge Therapy Decision (Warfarin)

| Risk Category | Decision |
|--------------|---------|
| High thrombotic risk (mechanical mitral valve, AF with CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥7, recent VTE) | Bridge with therapeutic LMWH or UFH |
| Low thrombotic risk (mechanical bileaflet aortic valve without AF, AF with low score, VTE >12 months ago) | No bridge; hold warfarin; restart postop |
| Intermediate | Individualize with prescribing clinician |

### Holding and Resuming DOACs for Surgery

| Risk of Bleeding | Hold DOAC Before Procedure |
|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Low bleeding risk (minor procedures, cataract, minor dental) | 24 hours before |
| High bleeding risk (major surgery, neuraxial anesthesia) | 48–72 hours (longer with renal impairment for dabigatran) |

| CrCl | Dabigatran hold (high-risk surgery) |
|------|-------------------------------------|
| ≥50 mL/min | 2–3 days |
| 30–50 mL/min | 4 days |
| <30 mL/min | 5+ days or avoid |

- Restart: 24–72 hours after hemostasis achieved, depending on bleeding risk

## Anticoagulation Reversal

| Agent | Reversal Drug | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| Warfarin | 4-factor PCC (Kcentra) + IV Vitamin K | Fastest; preferred over FFP |
| Dabigatran | Idarucizumab (Praxbind) | 5 g IV; complete reversal in minutes |
| Rivaroxaban/Apixaban/Edoxaban | Andexanet alfa (Andexxa) | Reverses anti-Xa activity; expensive; specific indications |
| Any DOAC | 4-factor PCC (off-label) | If specific reversal agent unavailable |
| UFH | Protamine sulfate | 1 mg per 100 units heparin given in last 2–2.5 h |
| LMWH | Protamine sulfate | Partial reversal (60–75%); 1 mg per 1 mg enoxaparin |
