# Clinical Guidelines: Diabetes Management

**Source:** American Diabetes Association (ADA) Standards of Care in Diabetes — 2024  
**Supplemented by:** AACE/ACE 2023 Comprehensive Diabetes Algorithm  
**APhA Reference Version:** 2024.1

---

## Classification of Diabetes

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Type 1 (T1D) | Autoimmune β-cell destruction; absolute insulin deficiency |
| Type 2 (T2D) | Progressive insulin secretory defect in setting of insulin resistance |
| Gestational (GDM) | Diagnosed in 2nd or 3rd trimester; not pre-existing |
| Other specific types | Monogenic, secondary (pancreatitis, Cushing's, medications) |

---

## Diagnostic Criteria

Any one of the following:

| Test | Diabetes | Prediabetes |
|------|----------|-------------|
| Fasting Plasma Glucose (FPG) | ≥126 mg/dL | 100–125 mg/dL |
| 2-h OGTT (75 g glucose) | ≥200 mg/dL | 140–199 mg/dL |
| HbA1c | ≥6.5% | 5.7–6.4% |
| Random glucose with symptoms | ≥200 mg/dL | — |

*In absence of unequivocal hyperglycemia, confirm with repeat testing.*

## Glycemic Targets

### Adults (Non-Pregnant)
| Target | General | Older/Complex | Young/Pregnant |
|--------|---------|---------------|----------------|
| HbA1c | <7.0% | <8.0% (higher OK if risk > benefit) | <6.5% (T1D in pregnancy) |
| Preprandial glucose | 80–130 mg/dL | — | 70–95 mg/dL |
| Peak postprandial glucose | <180 mg/dL | — | <120 mg/dL (1 h) |
| Time in Range (TIR 70–180) | ≥70% | ≥50% | ≥70% |
| Time Below Range (<70) | <4% | — | <4% |

### Pediatric and Adolescent
- HbA1c <7.0% (reasonable for most)
- Consider <6.5% if achievable without significant hypoglycemia

## Lifestyle Management

### Medical Nutrition Therapy (MNT)
- No single "diabetes diet" — individualize approach
- Reduce refined carbohydrates, added sugars, and saturated fat
- Mediterranean, DASH, or low-carbohydrate patterns all evidence-based
- Consistent carbohydrate intake at each meal helps with glycemic control
- Registered dietitian referral recommended

### Physical Activity
- Adults: ≥150 min/week moderate-intensity aerobic activity (or ≥75 min/week vigorous)
- Resistance training ≥2–3 days/week
- Limit prolonged sitting — break sedentary time every 30 minutes

### Weight Management (T2D)
- 5% weight loss improves glycemia; >10–15% may achieve remission in some patients
- Consider intensive lifestyle intervention, pharmacotherapy (GLP-1 RA, SGLT2i), or bariatric surgery for eligible patients

## Pharmacologic Management — Type 2 Diabetes

### First-Line
**Metformin** remains first-line in most patients (if tolerated and eGFR ≥30):
- Well-established efficacy, safety, weight neutrality, CV benefit, low cost
- If contraindicated or not tolerated → proceed to other evidence-based agents

### Agent Selection Based on Comorbidities (ADA 2024)

#### Established Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease (ASCVD)
- **Preferred:** GLP-1 RA with proven CV benefit (semaglutide, liraglutide, dulaglutide) OR SGLT2i with proven CV benefit (empagliflozin, canagliflozin, dapagliflozin)
- Add to metformin or use as first-line if HbA1c far from goal

#### Heart Failure (HFrEF or HFpEF)
- **Preferred:** SGLT2i (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) — reduce HF hospitalization
- Avoid thiazolidinediones (worsen fluid retention)

#### Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
- **Preferred:** SGLT2i (reduce GFR decline, albuminuria) + finerenone for diabetic CKD with albuminuria
- GLP-1 RA second-line for further CV/renal protection
- Metformin: hold if eGFR <30; reduce dose if eGFR 30–45

#### Weight Management Priority
- **Preferred:** GLP-1 RA (semaglutide > liraglutide) or dual GIP/GLP-1 RA (tirzepatide) — most weight loss
- Avoid: Insulin (weight gain), sulfonylureas (weight gain), thiazolidinediones (weight gain)

#### Hypoglycemia Risk Minimization
- **Preferred:** Metformin, GLP-1 RA, SGLT2i, DPP-4i — low hypoglycemia risk
- Avoid: Sulfonylureas, insulin (higher risk); if used, patient education critical

#### Cost Minimization
- Metformin + sulfonylurea (glipizide, glimepiride) — very low cost
- Generic pioglitazone (low cost, but caution with HF, fractures, bladder cancer)

### Insulin Therapy — Type 2 Diabetes

#### When to Initiate
- HbA1c ≥10–12% or symptomatic hyperglycemia (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss)
- Severe hyperglycemia (glucose >300–350 mg/dL consistently)
- Failure of 2–3 non-insulin agents at maximum tolerated doses

#### Insulin Types and Profiles

| Type | Examples | Onset | Peak | Duration |
|------|----------|-------|------|----------|
| Rapid-acting | Lispro (Humalog), Aspart (NovoLog), Glulisine (Apidra) | 5–15 min | 30–90 min | 3–5 h |
| Short-acting (Regular) | Humulin R, Novolin R | 30–60 min | 2–3 h | 5–8 h |
| Intermediate (NPH) | Humulin N, Novolin N | 2–4 h | 4–10 h | 10–18 h |
| Long-acting basal | Glargine (Lantus/Basaglar/Toujeo), Detemir (Levemir) | 1–2 h | Peakless/minimal | 20–24 h |
| Ultra-long-acting | Degludec (Tresiba) | 30–90 min | Peakless | >42 h |
| Ultra-rapid | Faster aspart (Fiasp), Lispro-aabc (Lyumjev) | 4 min | 60–90 min | 3–5 h |

#### Initiation Strategy
- **Basal-only:** Start with long-acting insulin (glargine or detemir)
  - 0.1–0.2 units/kg/day OR 10 units at bedtime
  - Titrate: increase by 2 units every 3 days until fasting glucose 80–130 mg/dL
- **Basal-bolus:** Add rapid-acting insulin to largest meal when basal alone inadequate

## Pharmacologic Management — Type 1 Diabetes

- Insulin is required (basal-bolus or continuous subcutaneous insulin infusion [CSII/pump])
- Carbohydrate counting for dose adjustment
- Adjunctive agents: Pramlintide (amylin analog), SGLT2 inhibitors (off-label; caution DKA risk), GLP-1 RA (off-label)
- CGM strongly recommended for all T1D patients

## Monitoring and Follow-Up

### Glycemic Monitoring
| Test | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| HbA1c | Every 3 months (not at goal) → Every 6 months (at goal, stable) |
| Fasting glucose (SMBG) | Daily–multiple times/day per regimen |
| Continuous Glucose Monitor (CGM) | Preferred for T1D and insulin-using T2D |
| Time in Range (TIR) | Target ≥70% for T2D; higher for T1D pregnancy |

### Complication Screening

| Complication | Test | Frequency |
|-------------|------|-----------|
| Nephropathy | Urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio + eGFR | Annually (T1D after 5 years; T2D from diagnosis) |
| Retinopathy | Dilated eye exam | At T1D diagnosis after 5 years; at T2D diagnosis; then annually |
| Neuropathy | Monofilament/vibration/pinprick foot exam | Annually; comprehensive every 1–2 years |
| ASCVD | BP, lipids, fasting glucose | At every visit (BP); annually (lipids if stable) |
| Thyroid (T1D) | TSH | At diagnosis; if symptomatic |

## Cardiovascular Risk Reduction

### Blood Pressure Targets
- General: <130/80 mmHg
- High CV risk: <130/80 mmHg
- Elderly frail patients: individualize

### Lipid Management
- Statin therapy for most adults >40 years (or <40 with ASCVD risk factors)
- LDL-C target: <70 mg/dL for established ASCVD; <100 mg/dL for others
- High-intensity statin preferred if ASCVD established

### Antiplatelet Therapy
- Aspirin 75–162 mg/day: Established ASCVD (secondary prevention)
- Primary prevention: Not recommended routinely (bleeding risk outweighs benefit in low-risk patients)

---

## Diabetes Technology

### Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGM)
- Recommended for all adults on intensive insulin regimens or T1D
- Real-time CGM (Dexcom G7, Libre 3) vs. intermittently-scanned CGM (FreeStyle Libre)
- Reduces HbA1c, hypoglycemia frequency, and improves TIR

### Insulin Pumps (CSII)
- Consider for patients with T1D not achieving targets with MDI
- Closed-loop systems (hybrid AID: Omnipod 5, Control-IQ) reduce HbA1c and hypoglycemia

## Hypoglycemia Management

### Classification
| Level | Glucose | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Level 1 | 54–69 mg/dL | Treat with 15 g fast-acting carbohydrate |
| Level 2 | <54 mg/dL | Treat; reassess cause |
| Level 3 | Any; cognitive impairment requiring assistance | Glucagon or IV dextrose |

### "Rule of 15"
- 15 g carbohydrate → recheck in 15 min → repeat if still <70 mg/dL → eat meal when >70 mg/dL
- Sources: 4 oz juice, 4 glucose tablets, 1 tbsp sugar, 8 oz low-fat milk

### Severe Hypoglycemia (Level 3)
- Prescribe glucagon kit for all insulin users (GlucaGen, Baqsimi nasal, Gvoke auto-injector)
- Educate family/household members on administration
