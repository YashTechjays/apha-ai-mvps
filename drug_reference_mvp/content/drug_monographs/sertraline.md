# Sertraline Hydrochloride

**Drug Class:** Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitor (SSRI)  
**Brand Names:** Zoloft  
**APhA Reference Version:** 2024.1

---

## Mechanism of Action

Sertraline selectively inhibits the presynaptic serotonin transporter (SERT), blocking reuptake of serotonin (5-HT) into the presynaptic neuron and increasing serotonin availability in the synaptic cleft. Unlike some SSRIs, sertraline also has weak dopamine transporter inhibition. Long-term receptor changes (downregulation of postsynaptic 5-HT receptors, increased BDNF) likely underlie the delayed therapeutic effect (2–4 weeks for full antidepressant effect).

---

## Indications

- **FDA-Approved:**
  - Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) — adults (age ≥18 for label; used in children/adolescents off-label/labeled for other indications)
  - Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) — adults and pediatric (age ≥6)
  - Panic Disorder (PD) — adults
  - Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) — adults
  - Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD) — adults
  - Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD) — adults

- **Off-Label (Common):**
  - Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD)
  - Premature ejaculation
  - Bulimia nervosa
  - Binge eating disorder
  - Pediatric depression

---

## Dosing

### Major Depressive Disorder (Adults)
- Start: 50 mg/day
- Usual therapeutic range: 50–200 mg/day
- Titrate: increase by 25–50 mg every 1–2 weeks based on response and tolerability
- Maximum: 200 mg/day

### OCD (Adults)
- Start: 50 mg/day
- Range: 50–200 mg/day

### OCD (Pediatric, 6–17 years)
- Age 6–12: Start 25 mg/day; max 200 mg/day
- Age 13–17: Start 50 mg/day; max 200 mg/day

### Panic Disorder, PTSD, SAAD
- Start: 25 mg/day × 1 week, then 50 mg/day
- Titrate as needed; max 200 mg/day

### PMDD
- **Continuous dosing:** 50–150 mg/day throughout cycle
- **Luteal phase dosing:** 50–150 mg/day starting 14 days before expected menses through first full day of menses

### Hepatic Impairment
- Use with caution; consider lower doses and/or less frequent dosing

---

## Contraindications

- Concurrent use with MAOIs (or within 14 days of stopping an MAOI); wait 14 days after stopping sertraline before starting an MAOI
- Concurrent use with pimozide
- Use with linezolid or IV methylene blue (serotonin syndrome risk)
- Hypersensitivity to sertraline

---

## Warnings and Precautions

### Suicidality (Black Box Warning)
Increased risk of suicidal thinking and behavior in children, adolescents, and young adults (18–24 years) in short-term studies. Monitor for worsening depression, unusual changes in behavior, or emergence of suicidal ideation, especially during initial weeks and after dose changes. **Does not apply to adults >24 years; may reduce suicidality in adults ≥65 years.**

### Serotonin Syndrome
Risk increased when combined with other serotonergic agents (triptans, tramadol, fentanyl, lithium, linezolid, St. John's Wort, other serotonergic antidepressants). Presents as agitation, hyperthermia, tachycardia, diaphoresis, myoclonus, hyperreflexia, GI symptoms, incoordination.

### SSRI Discontinuation Syndrome
Abrupt discontinuation may cause dizziness, nausea, sensory disturbances ("brain zaps"), irritability, anxiety, insomnia. Taper dose gradually to minimize symptoms.

### QTc Prolongation
Sertraline prolongs the QTc interval in a dose-related fashion. Use caution in patients with existing QT prolongation, hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, or concurrent QT-prolonging drugs.

### Hyponatremia (SIADH)
SSRIs can cause SIADH with hyponatremia; risk increased in elderly, volume-depleted patients, or those on thiazide diuretics.

### Bleeding Risk
SSRIs inhibit platelet serotonin uptake; combined with NSAIDs/aspirin/anticoagulants, increases risk of GI and other bleeding.

### Activation of Mania/Hypomania
Screen for bipolar disorder before initiating; can precipitate manic episodes in patients with undiagnosed bipolar disorder.

### Angle-Closure Glaucoma
Can precipitate acute angle-closure glaucoma in susceptible patients with untreated narrow-angle glaucoma.

## Drug Interactions

| Interacting Drug | Effect | Recommendation |
|-----------------|--------|----------------|
| MAOIs | Serotonin syndrome; potentially fatal | Contraindicated; allow 14-day washout each direction |
| Pimozide | ↑ pimozide levels (QT prolongation, arrhythmias) | Contraindicated |
| Linezolid, methylene blue IV | Serotonin syndrome | Contraindicated |
| Triptans (sumatriptan, rizatriptan) | Additive serotonergic effect | Monitor; use with caution |
| Tramadol | Serotonin syndrome; reduced seizure threshold | Avoid if possible |
| Warfarin | Antiplatelet effect of SSRI; variable effect on INR | Monitor INR |
| NSAIDs/aspirin | ↑ GI bleeding risk | Use with caution; consider PPI |
| CYP2D6 substrates (TCAs, antipsychotics, tamoxifen) | Sertraline moderately inhibits CYP2D6 | Reduce dose of substrate as needed |
| Lithium | ↑ serotonergic effects | Monitor lithium levels and for serotonin toxicity |
| Alcohol | CNS depression | Advise caution or avoidance |

## Pharmacokinetics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Bioavailability | ~44% (first-pass effect); food increases Cmax and AUC |
| Tmax | 4.5–8.4 h |
| Protein binding | ~98% |
| Metabolism | CYP2C19, CYP2C9, CYP2D6, CYP3A4; active metabolite desmethylsertraline (weak SSRI) |
| Half-life | ~26 h (sertraline); ~66 h (desmethylsertraline) |
| Elimination | Fecal (~40–45%) and renal (~40–45%, as metabolites) |

## Onset of Effect

| Outcome | Expected Timeline |
|---------|-------------------|
| Anxiety symptoms | 2–4 weeks |
| Sleep, energy, appetite | 1–2 weeks |
| Depressed mood, concentration | 4–6 weeks |
| Full antidepressant effect | 6–12 weeks |

---

## Monitoring Parameters

- Depressive symptoms and response (use validated scales: PHQ-9, HAM-D)
- Anxiety symptoms (GAD-7, PDSS, PTSD checklists)
- Suicidal ideation/behavior (especially first 4 weeks and after dose changes in youth)
- Sexual dysfunction
- Weight, sleep, appetite
- Sodium levels in high-risk patients (elderly on diuretics)
- Discontinuation symptoms if dose reduced or stopped

---

## Patient Counseling Points

1. Take sertraline once daily — with food if GI upset occurs (though may be taken with or without food).
2. Therapeutic effects develop gradually — allow at least 4–6 weeks before evaluating full response; do not stop if you don't see immediate improvement.
3. **Do not stop abruptly** — taper the dose as directed to avoid discontinuation symptoms (dizziness, "brain zaps," nausea).
4. Contact your provider immediately if you notice worsening depression, new or worsening anxiety, agitation, panic attacks, or thoughts of self-harm — especially in the first weeks of treatment or after dose changes.
5. Avoid alcohol (increases CNS depression and may worsen depression).
6. Inform your pharmacist/provider about all medications, including St. John's Wort (drug interaction risk).
7. Sexual side effects are common but often manageable — discuss with your provider if bothersome.
8. Use caution when driving or operating machinery until you know how sertraline affects you.
9. May take several months of treatment for full benefit; continue therapy as directed even when feeling well.

## Special Populations

| Population | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| Pregnancy | Risk-benefit discussion — untreated depression is also harmful; neonatal adaptation syndrome (transient) with late-pregnancy use; possible small ↑ in cardiac septal defects (data conflicting) |
| Lactation | Low levels in breast milk; generally preferred SSRI for breastfeeding |
| Elderly | Higher risk of hyponatremia, falls, GI bleeding; start 25 mg; monitor closely |
| Pediatric (MDD) | FDA black box warning; nevertheless widely used — closely monitor especially first weeks |
| Hepatic impairment | Use lower doses; longer titration intervals |
| CYP2C19 poor metabolizers | Higher sertraline exposure; consider dose reduction |

---

## Switching Between Antidepressants

- **SSRI to SSRI:** Direct switch or overlap with brief taper
- **SSRI to SNRI:** Direct switch or brief cross-taper
- **SSRI to TCA:** 14-day washout not required but careful cross-taper (CYP2D6 interaction)
- **SSRI to MAOI:** Must allow **14 days** after stopping sertraline before starting MAOI
