# Metformin Hydrochloride

**Drug Class:** Biguanide Antidiabetic Agent  
**Brand Names:** Glucophage, Glucophage XR, Glumetza, Fortamet, Riomet  
**APhA Reference Version:** 2024.1

---

## Mechanism of Action

Metformin decreases hepatic glucose production primarily by inhibiting mitochondrial glycerophosphate dehydrogenase and reducing hepatic gluconeogenesis. It also activates AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), which increases glucose uptake in peripheral tissues and reduces lipogenesis. Metformin does not stimulate insulin secretion and does not cause hypoglycemia when used as monotherapy.

---

## Indications

- **FDA-Approved:** Type 2 diabetes mellitus (adjunct to diet and exercise)
- **Off-Label:** Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), prevention of type 2 diabetes in high-risk individuals, antipsychotic-induced weight gain

---

## Dosing

### Adults — Type 2 Diabetes
- **Immediate-Release (IR):** Start 500 mg twice daily with meals OR 850 mg once daily with a meal. Titrate by 500 mg/week or 850 mg every 2 weeks. Maximum: 2,550 mg/day in divided doses.
- **Extended-Release (XR):** Start 500–1,000 mg once daily with evening meal. Titrate by 500 mg/week. Maximum: 2,000–2,500 mg/day (product-dependent).

### Pediatric (≥10 years) — Type 2 Diabetes
- IR: 500 mg twice daily; increase by 500 mg/week. Maximum: 2,000 mg/day.
- XR formulations not approved in pediatric patients.

### Renal Dose Adjustment
| eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Recommendation |
|----------------------|----------------|
| ≥45 | No adjustment required |
| 30–44 | Use with caution; reduce dose if needed; monitor renal function every 3–6 months |
| <30 | Contraindicated |

---

## Contraindications

- eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²
- Acute or chronic metabolic acidosis (including diabetic ketoacidosis)
- Hypersensitivity to metformin

**Hold before:** iodinated contrast procedures (hold at time of procedure; restart 48 hours after if renal function remains stable)

---

## Warnings and Precautions

### Lactic Acidosis (Black Box Warning)
Risk is increased in renal impairment, hepatic impairment, congestive heart failure, and excessive alcohol use. Monitor renal function before initiating and periodically thereafter. Discontinue if lactic acidosis is suspected (symptoms: malaise, myalgia, respiratory distress, abdominal pain, hypothermia, bradyarrhythmias).

### Vitamin B12 Deficiency
Long-term metformin use is associated with decreased vitamin B12 absorption. Monitor B12 levels every 2–3 years in patients on long-term therapy.

### Radiologic Studies with Contrast
Withhold metformin at time of contrast injection; do not restart until 48 hours post-procedure and only after confirming renal function is stable.

---

## Drug Interactions

| Interacting Drug | Interaction | Management |
|-----------------|-------------|------------|
| Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors (topiramate, acetazolamide) | Increased risk of lactic acidosis | Use with caution; monitor |
| Alcohol | Increased risk of lactic acidosis | Counsel to minimize alcohol |
| Cimetidine | Increases metformin AUC by 40% | Monitor; may need dose reduction |
| Iodinated contrast media | Risk of contrast-induced nephropathy → lactic acidosis | Hold metformin at time of procedure |
| Insulin/secretagogues | Additive hypoglycemic risk | Adjust doses; counsel on hypoglycemia recognition |
| OCT2 inhibitors (dolutegravir, vandetanib) | Increased metformin plasma concentrations | Reduce metformin dose; monitor |

---

## Adverse Effects

**Common (>5%):**
- GI: Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, abdominal discomfort (usually transient; reduced by taking with food or using slow titration)
- Metallic taste

**Serious (<1%):**
- Lactic acidosis (rare but potentially fatal; ~3 cases/100,000 patient-years)
- Vitamin B12 deficiency (long-term use)

**Weight:** Metformin is weight-neutral to modestly weight-reducing.

---

## Pharmacokinetics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Bioavailability | 50–60% (IR); lower with XR |
| Tmax | 2.5 h (IR); 7 h (XR) |
| Protein binding | Negligible |
| Distribution | Vd ~654 L |
| Metabolism | Not hepatically metabolized |
| Elimination | Renal tubular secretion; t½ ~4–9 h (plasma) |

---

## Monitoring Parameters

- Renal function (SCr/eGFR): Before initiation; every 3–6 months if eGFR 30–60; annually if eGFR >60
- HbA1c: Every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months
- Vitamin B12: Baseline, then every 2–3 years
- Signs/symptoms of lactic acidosis

---

## Patient Counseling Points

1. Take with food to reduce GI side effects.
2. GI upset often improves after the first few weeks; do not stop without talking to your pharmacist/physician.
3. Avoid excessive alcohol use.
4. Notify your pharmacist before any imaging procedure requiring contrast dye.
5. This medication does not cause low blood sugar on its own, but may contribute when combined with insulin or sulfonylureas.
6. Extended-release tablets/capsules may appear whole in the stool — this is normal (ghost tablet phenomenon).

## Special Populations

| Population | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| Pregnancy (Category B historical) | Generally preferred first-line oral agent; crosses placenta; individual risk-benefit |
| Lactation | Low levels in breast milk; compatible with breastfeeding |
| Elderly | Use caution; check eGFR before initiating; start low dose |
| Hepatic impairment | Avoid in significant hepatic impairment (risk of lactic acidosis) |
