# Warfarin Sodium

**Drug Class:** Vitamin K Antagonist (VKA) Anticoagulant  
**Brand Names:** Coumadin, Jantoven  
**APhA Reference Version:** 2024.1

---

## Mechanism of Action

Warfarin inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase (VKOR), the enzyme that recycles vitamin K to its active reduced form. Vitamin K is a cofactor for the gamma-carboxylation of clotting factors II (prothrombin), VII, IX, and X, as well as anticoagulant proteins C and S. Inhibition results in the synthesis of inactive clotting factors with reduced biological activity. Onset of anticoagulant effect is delayed 2–5 days (time required for circulating active factors to be cleared).

---

## Indications

- **FDA-Approved:**
  - Venous thromboembolism (DVT and PE) — treatment and prevention
  - Atrial fibrillation (AF) — stroke and systemic embolism prevention
  - Mechanical heart valve thromboprophylaxis
  - Post-myocardial infarction (reduce risk of death, recurrent MI, thromboembolic events)

---

## Dosing

### Initiation and Titration
- Individualized dosing based on:
  - Target INR range (see table)
  - Patient factors: age, body weight, baseline INR, CYP2C9/VKORC1 genotype, diet, interacting medications
- **Typical initiation:** 2–5 mg/day; lower doses (1–2 mg) in elderly, debilitated, or those at high bleeding risk
- **Pharmacogenomic dosing** (CYP2C9 and VKORC1): Can predict dose requirements and reduce time to stable INR

### Target INR Ranges

| Indication | Target INR |
|------------|-----------|
| Most VTE (DVT/PE) | 2.0–3.0 |
| Atrial fibrillation | 2.0–3.0 |
| Bioprosthetic mitral valve | 2.0–3.0 (first 3 months) |
| Mechanical aortic valve (bileaflet/tilting disc) | 2.0–3.0 |
| Mechanical mitral valve | 2.5–3.5 |
| High-risk mechanical valves (older generation, AF, multiple valves) | 2.5–3.5 |
| Antiphospholipid antibody syndrome (high-risk) | 2.0–3.0 or 2.5–3.5 per risk |

### Duration of Therapy

| Indication | Duration |
|------------|----------|
| Provoked DVT/PE (transient risk factor) | 3 months |
| Unprovoked DVT/PE | At least 3–6 months; consider indefinite |
| Cancer-associated VTE | Prefer LMWH or DOAC; anticoagulate while cancer active |
| AF (CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2 men / ≥3 women) | Indefinite (unless contraindication) |
| Mechanical heart valves | Indefinite |

---

## Contraindications

- Active bleeding or significant hemorrhagic risk (intracranial, GI, etc.)
- Severe thrombocytopenia (<50,000/mm³)
- Pregnancy (Category X — teratogenic; can cause warfarin embryopathy and fetal hemorrhage)
- Recent neurosurgery or ophthalmic surgery
- Inability to perform INR monitoring

---

## Warnings and Precautions

### Bleeding (Black Box Warning)
Risk of fatal or serious bleeding. Factors increasing bleeding risk:
- INR above therapeutic range
- Older age
- History of bleeding (GI, intracranial)
- Uncontrolled hypertension
- Drug interactions
- Alcohol use
- Recent surgery or trauma

### Tissue Necrosis
Rare; typically occurs within first week of therapy; due to protein C depletion before anticoagulant effect; present as painful skin lesions. Avoid large loading doses; bridge with heparin.

### Systemic Atheroemboli / Cholesterol Microemboli
"Purple toe syndrome" — painful blue/purple discoloration of toes, feet, or other extremities. Discontinue warfarin if this occurs.

## Drug Interactions

Warfarin has hundreds of documented drug interactions. Key categories:

### Drugs That INCREASE INR (bleeding risk ↑)
| Drug/Class | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Amiodarone | CYP2C9/CYP3A4 inhibition; persists weeks after stopping |
| Fluconazole | CYP2C9 inhibition |
| Metronidazole | CYP2C9 inhibition |
| Trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole | CYP2C9 inhibition |
| Ciprofloxacin/fluoroquinolones | Reduced GI flora (vitamin K synthesis) |
| Aspirin / NSAIDs | Additive antiplatelet/GI bleeding risk |
| Thyroid hormone | Increased catabolism of vitamin K–dependent factors |
| Fish oil (high dose) | Antiplatelet activity |

### Drugs That DECREASE INR (thrombosis risk ↑)
| Drug/Class | Mechanism |
|-----------|-----------|
| Rifampin | Potent CYP2C9/CYP3A4 induction |
| Carbamazepine | CYP induction |
| Phenytoin | CYP induction (can also increase initially) |
| St. John's Wort | CYP induction |
| Cholestyramine | Reduced absorption |
| High vitamin K intake | Substrate competition |

### Dietary Interactions
- Vitamin K–rich foods (leafy greens: kale, spinach, collards) can significantly reduce INR
- **Counsel for consistency** (not avoidance) — sudden changes in dietary vitamin K intake alter INR
- Grapefruit juice: minor CYP3A4 inhibition

## Reversal Agents

| Situation | Agent | Dose/Notes |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| Asymptomatic supratherapeutic INR (4–10, no bleeding) | Hold warfarin ± oral vitamin K₁ | 1–2.5 mg PO vitamin K |
| INR >10, no significant bleeding | Oral vitamin K₁ | 2.5–5 mg PO; recheck in 24 h |
| Serious/life-threatening bleeding | 4-factor PCC (Kcentra) + IV vitamin K₁ | PCC: dose based on INR and weight; vitamin K 10 mg IV slow infusion |
| Urgent reversal (surgery, hemorrhage) | 4-factor PCC + IV vitamin K₁ | Faster than FFP; preferred over 3-factor PCC |
| FFP | Fresh frozen plasma | 2–4 units; slower and larger volume than PCC |

| Effect | Notes |

| Purple toe syndrome | Rare |
| Alopecia | Uncommon |
| Nausea, diarrhea | Uncommon |

## Pharmacokinetics

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Bioavailability | ~100% (oral) |
| Protein binding | ~99% (albumin) |
| Metabolism | CYP2C9 (S-warfarin; 3–5× more potent); CYP3A4, CYP1A2 (R-warfarin) |
| Half-life | 20–60 h (mean ~40 h); racemic mixture |
| Elimination | Renal (inactive metabolites) |

---

## Monitoring Parameters

- **INR:** At baseline; daily–every few days during initiation until stable; every 4 weeks once stable
- **INR frequency criteria for every 12-week monitoring:** Stable INR in therapeutic range for ≥6 months
- Signs/symptoms of bleeding (bruising, blood in urine/stool, unusual prolonged bleeding)
- Signs/symptoms of thrombosis (if sub-therapeutic)
- Hematocrit/CBC if significant bleeding suspected

---

## Patient Counseling Points

1. Take warfarin at the same time each day. If you miss a dose, take it as soon as you remember that same day; do not double-dose.
2. Keep all INR monitoring appointments — dose adjustments depend on your results.
3. Be consistent with your diet; do not suddenly increase or decrease vitamin K–rich foods (leafy greens).
4. Avoid alcohol or limit to 1–2 drinks/day maximum.
5. Tell ALL healthcare providers (including dentists) that you take warfarin before any procedure.
6. Avoid aspirin and NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) unless specifically directed; use acetaminophen for pain relief.
7. Seek immediate medical care for unusual bleeding (heavy periods, blood in urine/stool, vomiting blood, severe headache, vision changes, fall with head injury).
8. Carry a medical alert card/ID indicating warfarin use.
9. Contact your pharmacist before starting any new prescription or OTC medication, supplement, or herbal product.

## Special Populations

| Population | Consideration |
|-----------|---------------|
| Pregnancy | **CONTRAINDICATED** (embryopathy in 1st trimester; fetal CNS/hemorrhage throughout) — use LMWH |
| Lactation | Appears safe; monitor infant |
| Elderly | Increased sensitivity; higher bleeding risk; start 2–5 mg; more frequent INR monitoring |
| Renal impairment | INR not directly affected; higher bleeding risk; monitor more closely |
| Hepatic impairment | Reduced clotting factor synthesis → elevated baseline INR; use with extreme caution |

---

## DOACs vs. Warfarin Comparison

For most indications (AF, VTE), direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs: apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, dabigatran) are preferred over warfarin due to:
- Predictable pharmacokinetics (no routine INR monitoring)
- Fewer drug and food interactions
- Comparable or superior efficacy/safety in clinical trials

**Warfarin preferred in:**
- Mechanical heart valves
- Significant mitral stenosis with AF
- Severe renal impairment (CrCl <15–25 mL/min, depending on DOAC)
- Antiphospholipid antibody syndrome (triple positive)
- Where cost or access to DOACs is a barrier
